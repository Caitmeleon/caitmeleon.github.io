Gardening

It's winter now, and looking at the foot of snow on the ground, I can't wait for spring again. Last year, we were able to set up a small raised bed and planted peppers, tomatoes, strawberries and some herbs.

We have a chance to build a greenhouse this year! We've been doing research and currently I am leaning towards either an asymetrical style roof with the corrugated double walled plastic and a ~3ft kneewall or a sunken greenhouse. The sunken greenhouse would be more costly, at least up front. The location where we want to put the greenhouse already has a delapidated shed, so the ground work would be mostly completed. A sunken greenhouse, as the name implies, would require a hole to be dug in order to sink the greenhouse in to. We have many large rocks, buried and exposed, that will prove a nuisance as well.

In the next few years, we hope to start some small fruit/nut trees as well. We've been looking into trees that would grow well in our area and making some lists of ones that we're interested in (apples, pears, cherries, etc).
